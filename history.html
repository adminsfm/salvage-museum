The Story of Salvage

For generations, Salvage has been shaped by the sea. Fishing, boatbuilding, craft, family traditions, and community life created a unique outport story.

The Salvage Fisherman's Museum preserves these stories through artifacts, photographs, documents and memories.

Early Settlement

Families arrived and built their lives around the fisheries of Newfoundland's coast.

Fishing and Community

The harbour became a centre for fishing, trade and daily life.

1969

The historic house became the Salvage Fisherman's Museum.

Heritage Preservation

The museum continues to protect the stories of Salvage for future generations.
